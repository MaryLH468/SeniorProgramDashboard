Cognitive Load Theory — John Sweller
Sweller's Cognitive Load Theory
Distinguishes intrinsic, extraneous and germane cognitive load. Emphasises working memory limitations in learning. Key for designing low-load tasks for our YP.
Principles of Instruction — Barak Rosenshine
Rosenshine's Principles of Instruction
Includes opportunities to respond (OTR) within explicit instruction. Highlights guided practice and checking for understanding as core to effective teaching.
Formative Assessment — Dylan Wiliam
Dylan Wiliam — Checking for Understanding
Frames CFU as a core component of formative assessment. Emphasises responsive teaching based on evidence of learning.
Explicit Instruction
Explicit Instruction — Archer & Hughes
Framework for clear, direct instruction with worked examples, guided practice and independent work. Essential for our diverse learners.
Trauma-Informed Practice
Trauma-Informed & Strength-Based Approaches
Guides for trauma-aware teaching, strength-based framing, and low-anxiety remote learning environments for our YP.
